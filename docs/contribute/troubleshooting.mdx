Most "Claude is being weird" moments have a simple cause. Here are the common ones and the fix.

## "It made up a fact / fake source"
**Why:** the model generated a plausible-but-false continuation ([hallucination](/docs/foundations/hallucinations)). **Fix:** give it the source text and say *"answer only from this; if it's not here, say so."* Verify anything that matters.

## "It forgot what I said earlier"
**Why:** the conversation outgrew the [context window](/docs/foundations/tokens-and-context), so older text dropped off — or it's a new session (no [memory](/docs/claude-app/memory) by default). **Fix:** restate the key info, or start a fresh chat for a new topic.

## "It ignored my instruction"
**Why:** the instruction was buried in a long prompt, ambiguous, or contradicted by something else. **Fix:** put the instruction up top *and* restate it at the end; be specific; remove conflicting asks. See [Prompting Basics](/docs/prompting/basics).

## "It refused something reasonable"
**Why:** the request pattern-matched to something sensitive, or context was missing. **Fix:** add legitimate context and be specific about your goal. If you're building on the API, handle refusals gracefully — see [Refusals & Safety](/docs/api/refusals-and-safety).

## "The output is generic / bland"
**Why:** not enough constraints or no example of what "good" looks like. **Fix:** give it a [role](/docs/prompting/pattern-library), a sample of the voice you want, and a tight format.

## "The answer was wrong on numbers/math"
**Why:** LLMs reason about math but aren't exact calculators. **Fix:** ask it to show steps, or give it a [tool](/docs/api/tool-use)/code to compute.

## "It won't stop being verbose / too brief"
**Fix:** say exactly what you want — *"answer in 3 bullets, no preamble"* or *"give me the full reasoning."* In Claude Code, consider [Output Styles](/docs/claude-code/output-styles).

## "Claude Code did something I didn't expect to my files"
**Why:** broad [permissions](/docs/claude-code/permissions) let it act without asking. **Fix:** use [Plan Mode](/docs/claude-code/plan-mode) for big changes and tighten your allow/deny rules.

:::tip The universal first move
Before assuming it's broken, **re-read your prompt as if you were a literal stranger**. Nine times out of ten the fix is more context or a clearer ask.
:::
